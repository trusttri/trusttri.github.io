Some very quickly written thoughts about thinking too much...

---

I'm hesitant to upload this, but it feels like I've been living in my head a lot these days. That's probably not news, though, but what's new is that I've reflected on more about how I feel about it. I actually think living in one's head is not always bad -- I can sense it has been helpful for research. But, it's also true I could've used my time better, instead of... just thinking and daydreaming. And, it can be immensely exhausting. Like many things, finding the right balance is art.

The good news is that compared to years ago, I can sense better when my thoughts start to drown me. I wonder if PhDing helped -- after all, finishing a PhD requires a ton of metacognition. Compared to before I started the PhD, and last year, even, I can understand myself much better. In very nuanced ways, I can more clearly see what I'm thinking and feeling, and tell myself, "Oh, so that's where you were heading towards. Time to stop here, Jane."

Still, I wish I could immaculately master the skill of knowing what and how to think at a given time. I wonder if finding a "proper" hobby will help. The goal would be to periodically and intensely focus on something other than my own thoughts that are eating at me. To be clear, I (think) know what to do to truly relax and let go of negative energy -- journaling, hanging out with people I deeply trust, listening to music, running, reading, etc. But, when I journal, I mostly write about my thoughts, which would require me to reflect on them. And, there will be moments when I want, or need, to calm myself down, instead of spending time with others. The only problem with listening to music is that I can go on forever (and be in a daydream mode). Running helps me live in the present, but I want something more artistic. Reading is soothing, but I want something more active.

At this point, I wonder if it's just me wanting something different, ha ha ha! :] I've been telling myself I need a new hobby since last year, and the good news is that I did start reading more books. But, I have a nagging feeling I should try to do something more... active? Not too academic? Hmm. There have been a couple of candidates, but no success so far. I've been daydreaming about starting a hobby instead of actually doing it. This is also not new to my family and friends, probably.
