This project is still ongoing, so I will briefly introduce my motivation, contribution, and things I learned so far from this project.


Motivation

Experiencing millions of people protesting against the political corruption in South Korea (2016 - 2017) and witnessing it lead to the impeachment of the previous president was the main motivation.

It was surprising to me that the protests stemmed out from discussions in social media. I wanted to study more about systems that can build consensus on a large scale, further aiding collective action, and asked for voluntary research opportunities in Haystack Group at MIT.


Contribution

My main contribution to this project was (i) to create a novel dataset of RfCs, (ii) analyze the dataset to earn insights about the RfC process, and (iii) implement new features in Wikum to improve the process.


What I learned

I found it interesting to (i) earn insights about online discussion through mining data and (ii) develop systems to improve collaborative work.

Furthermore, I began to have interest in resolving disputes and bridging opposing views online.
