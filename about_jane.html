Hi! I am currently an undergraduate in Korea University, double majoring Business and Computer Science.


I have studied and engaged in three research projects in MIT EECS (course 6) by applying through undergraduate special student program from fall 2016 to spring 2017.


Due to my interdisciplinary background of studying Computer Science and Business together, I found it natural for me to feel passion in research that uses computation while also considering the social context.


Currently I am applying for graduate schools, wanting to study more about human-computer interaction and related systems.


Why Computer Science? Why HCI research?

Although I started off as a Business major, I always had the thirst for creation using technology. I looked at the programmers more in awe instead of the project managers.

This led me to learn programming and lead a team in Big Data Analytics Competition to design a business plan from analyzing big data.
I greatly enjoyed the process of teaching myself how to preprocess the data, select the right algorithms, and analyze the result for 3 months.

However, I realized I liked projects with a more novel and fundamental approach than designing business plans, and developed a curiosity for research within Computer Science.

At the same time, I fully knew that I was interested in the interaction between the computer and the human, attributing to a user-centered mindset I gained from Business classes. This led me to decide to involve in more human-centric research projects.
